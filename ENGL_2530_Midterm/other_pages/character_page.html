Some of the Characters in Beowulf and how they are important to the story.

Beowulf: He is important to the story he is one of the major enforcers of the heroic ideal. Many other "good guys" in the story would like to enforce it and try, but Beowulf is the only one that is able to actually go through with it up until the "third act". In that part Wiglaf comes close when he runs in to try to help defend Beowulf from the dragon and as well as when he scolds the rest of Beowulf's retainers for just running away when Beowulf needed them. The reason that this matters to today's audience he is a type of warning to not let your pride get the best of you and cloud your judgement.

Unferth: He is important to emphasize how amazing Beowulf is, we can see this leading up to Beowulf 's fight with Grendel where he cast doubt on how powerful Beowulf is by mentioning another character, that is only talked about once, Breca and how Beowulf lost swimming race. Beowulf then "sets the record straight" by telling everyone that he was doing a multitude of other things other than just swimming. The main reason that this matters today is the way that Beowulf handled this "heckler" which was with peace and grace he just calmly and quickly dispersed Unferth's claims and them cast doubt on Unferth's character.

Grendel: Grendel is important because he is the first conflict, we see Beowulf deal with. Due to this he is built up as this super strong and overwhelming monster that has been terrorizing Heorot for going on twelve winters. With this build up the reader gets the sense that Grendel is ultimately unstoppable. So, when Beowulf comes in and easily defeats Grendel, the reader begins to grasp the strength that Beowulf has. The way that this can be meaningful to people today is the idea that no matter how difficult/impossible something might seem it is still possible to overcome the difficult hurdles.

Grendel's Mother: Grendel's Mother is one of the few people in the poem that appear to use her intelligence combined with her strength. Grendel when he started his attacks, he was fueled primarily rage and from what we gather from the way acted when Beowulf confronted him that Grendel just runs in and starts attacking. It seems that he never really considered, "Maybe they will be able to defeat me this time." Whereas with his mother, she did not really try to "sneak in" per se, but when she was caught she grabbed essentially a hostage and retreated. The reason that she retreated is up for debate that being either in fright of being overwhelmed or as a bait for Beowulf, who may believe was the desired target when coming to the Heorot, to make him come to her lair where she would have more of an advantage. The way that this has an importance with people today is to try and use you advantages when it is possible because you have move experience.
